How Has The Media Twisted The Perception We Have On Vodou?

Kendall D, Amulya P, Salvatore Z, May 14, 2015 | 1 Comment
  • Print
  • Share:

Do Now

1. Based on the commercialized perception of Vodou rituals, why should you educate others on the reality of Vodou?

2. How could you take a step to stop others from practicing the Vodou for its entertainment or tourist value?

3. How has the media formed or shaped the impression and view that people have on Voodoo? Why is it important to understand the mediaâ€™s role in relaying information and beliefs?

Documentary photographer Paccarik Orue worked on a project called Thereâ€™s Nothing Beautiful Around Here in Richmond, California, a city with a reputation for being unsafe. The title of his project hints at the misconceptions of Richmond, because he actually found many beautiful, positive moments with his camera.

He explored the community for two years, meeting people and making photographs of people and places he found beautiful. He comments on what he calls â€œthe golden light of the [San Francisco] Bay Area,â€ which he uses to his advantage to create beautiful images. The idea of beauty is subjective and can be found anywhere, and isnâ€™t limited to aesthetic values or superficial notions. Beauty can describe things like actions, feelings and arrangements, and can be achieved through composition and juxtapositions.

Use your camera to snap a detail, a moment, or anything that you personally think is beautiful. There are no rules and restrictions, you can find something that appeals to you in a rusty gate, a colorful pile of paper, or a macro detail of an everyday object. Consider composition and lighting when framing your image.

Paccarik Orue uses film cameras to slow himself down so that he has time to think and reflect on what he is seeing. Instead of taking multiple shots and editing them later, give yourself only one or two tries to capture an image that feels positive and appealing to your eye. Spend some time thinking of a caption, then post a Twitpic of your image with a thoughtful title and #DoNowVodou.

More Resources

VIDEO: Is Instagram the Best Thing to Ever Happen to Photography? (PBS Idea Channel)
With its ability to make boring cellphone photos look “vintage” and “artsy”, Instagram has exploded worldwide. Derided by its detractors as a tool for making bad photos worse, we take an alternate view and argue that Instagram is the greatest thing to ever happen to photography. Its simple filters and social networking features are training cellphone photographers everywhere to think creatively about their photos. Plus, the app is turning its worldwide user base into an army of photojournalists capturing striking images of the people and events around them. As the old photography adage goes, “The best camera is the one you have with you.”

VIDEO: Photo a Friend – Tanja Hollander (The Art Assignment)
We met up with photographer Tanja Hollander in North Adams, MA, on her epic journey around the world to visit and take formal portraits of ALL of her 626 facebook friends: http://www.areyoureallymyfriend.com/. She challenges you to take a formal photographic portrait of a friend, and to tell us what a real friend is.

VIDEO: Printmaking for Activism with Favianna Rodriguez (KQED Art School)
Favianna Rodriguez is a visual artist and community organizer who merges her artistic practice with political activism. Through her bold, vivid artwork, she has become a leading voice in raising awareness about immigration, women’s issues, globalization and economic injustice.

About the Authors

Kendall D. is a current student at New Tech High @ Coppell. She is involved with PBS Newshour Student Reporting Labs and a variety of activities regarding writing and film. She is interested in majoring in Biology at University of Washington

Amulya P. is a student at New Tech High @ Coppell. She has been working with KQED and PBS NewsHour for nearly a year. Amulya has also contributed to Student Reporting Labs and has been recognized for high performance by her facilitator. Currently she is working on a short film about social mentality of bullying and is devoted to untangling this issue.

Salvatore Z. is a student at New Tech High @ Coppell. He has been recognized by his teachers for advanced performance in the classroom and is interested in pursuing a career in film scoring.

Do Now

Do Now is a weekly activity for students to engage and respond to current issues using social media tools like Twitter. Do Now aims to build civic engagement and digital literacy for young folks.
